4-Day Women’s Gym Plan

My Workout Plan

Workout plan for a toned, hourglass-focused physique with priority on glutes, shoulders, arms, and core.

Rules Day 1 Day 2 Day 3 Day 4 Progression

Global Training Rules

Main liftsUse 3–4 sets of 8–12 reps with 90–120 seconds rest.
Isolation liftsUse 3 sets of 12–20 reps with 45–75 seconds rest.
Effort targetFinish most sets with 1–2 reps left in reserve.
Progressive overloadWhen all sets reach the top of the rep range with good form, increase the weight next session.
TempoLower slowly for about 2–3 seconds, lift with control, and pause where noted.
Warm-upDo 5–8 minutes of light cardio, mobility, and 1–2 lighter warm-up sets before the first big lifts.

Simple Weekly Progression Rules

Stay in the target rep rangeDo not rush weight increases before the reps and form are stable.
Keep 1–2 reps in reserveMost sets should feel challenging but not like an absolute max effort.
Increase load only when earnedOnce all sets reach the top of the range with good form, move the weight up next time.
If form breaksKeep the same weight next session and clean up the technique first.
Example: if Hip Thrust is 4 × 8–12, stay with the same load until all 4 sets reach 12 with strong form. Then increase the weight next session.

Typical weekly layout: Monday Day 1, Tuesday Day 2, Thursday Day 3, Saturday Day 4.
